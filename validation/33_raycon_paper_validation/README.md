# Raycon Paper Validation

This case compares the Julia Raycon implementation with the three local paper
references in the workspace sibling `ref/` directory:

- `jaun2006.pdf`: Jaun et al., "Eikonal waves, caustics, and mode conversion in
  tokamak plasmas", PPCF 49 (2007), Figs. 3-10.
- `tracy2001.pdf`: Tracy et al., "A ray-based algorithm for multi-dimensional
  linear conversion", Physics Letters A 290 (2001), especially Eqs. (20)-(22)
  and Fig. 4.
- `tracy2007.pdf`: Tracy, Kaufman, and Jaun, "Local fields for asymptotic
  matching in multidimensional mode conversion", Physics of Plasmas 14 (2007),
  especially Eqs. (54)-(58).

The case deliberately separates exact theory identities from figure-derived
paper comparisons:

- Exact Tracy checks: `tau = exp(-pi*eta2)`, S-matrix unitarity
  `tau^2 + abs2(beta) = 1`, and the transmitted ray landing on `U = 0`.
- Jaun Fig. 3/Fig. 4 mid-plane checks: conversion count, first transmitted and
  incoming phase-space points, first/second transmission coefficients, coupling,
  and the symmetry of the two mid-plane transmissions.
- Jaun Fig. 6/Fig. 7 off-mid-plane checks: the fixed lab-frame launch
  `(R,Z,kR,kZ)=(3.45,-0.31,-27,0)`, the two conversions, the first incoming
  `kZ`, and both quoted transmission coefficients.
- Jaun Fig. 9/Fig. 10 D(20%H) strong-coupling checks: the JET setup with
  hydrogen substituted for helium, `B0=3.0 T`, and the upstream `ray05`
  H-profile choice `naH=0.5`; the case gates the conversion count, the first
  two quoted coupling strengths, the minimum strong-coupling value among the
  first four conversions, and the quoted negligible transmission.

The Jaun targets are taken from the paper text and visual inspection of Figs. 3,
4, 6, 7, 9, and 10. Tolerances are therefore loose for coordinates and
figure/text transmission values,
but tight for the exact Tracy identities.

This case is non-default. It is expected to be run explicitly:

```bash
julia --project=validation validation/run_validation.jl --case 33_raycon_paper_validation
```

Generated artifacts include:

- `33_raycon_paper_validation.csv`: gated scalar metrics.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_conversions.csv`: per-conversion phase-space,
  coupling, and validity diagnostics.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_phase_space.csv`: computed and paper target
  `(R,kR)` points for plotting.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_phase_space.pdf`: Fig. 3-style overlay generated
  by `plot.jl`.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_fig6_7_conversions.csv`: Fig. 6/7 off-mid-plane
  conversion diagnostics.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_fig6_7_rz.csv`: Fig. 6/7 computed `(R,Z)` trace
  and paper launch target.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_fig6_7_rz.pdf`: Fig. 6/7-style R-Z overlay
  generated by `plot.jl`.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_fig9_10_dh_conversions.csv`: Fig. 9/10 D-H
  conversion diagnostics.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_fig9_10_dh_rz.csv`: Fig. 9/10 computed `(R,Z)`
  trace and launch target.
- `33_raycon_paper_validation_fig9_10_dh_rz.pdf`: Fig. 9/10-style R-Z overlay
  generated by `plot.jl`.

The Jaun Fig. 3/Fig. 4 gate uses the historical upstream `jet06` setup
(`iaspr=0.3`, `ne=7.0e19 m^-3`, `nD=5.6e19 m^-3`, `nHe3=1.4e19 m^-3`) because
that preset reproduces the plotted launch and mid-plane conversion trajectory.
The later quasi-neutral density edit (`ne=nD+2*nHe3`) projects the same requested
launch onto a different dispersion-surface point and is not the figure-matching
benchmark used here.

The Jaun Fig. 9/Fig. 10 gate uses the upstream D-H figure-matching profile
choice `naH=0.5`. Replaying the same launch with `naH=1.0` gives a weak second
event (`tau` about `0.505`) instead of the paper's negligible transmission.

Every paper comparison in this case is represented by a real validation metric.
Missing conversion events or out-of-tolerance paper comparisons are reported as
validation failures.
